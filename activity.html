#SriLanka: Economic Crisis Puts Rights in Peril

Sri Lanka’s economic crisis is driving millions of people into poverty, jeopardizing their rights to health, education, and an adequate standard of living, Human Rights Watch said today (18). The Sri Lankan government should work with relevant financial institutions and partners to establish a new social protection system and obtain debt relief, adopt measures to ensure fair taxation, and address corruption at the highest levels of government Large-scale protests against economic mismanagement, corruption, and human rights abuses since March 2022, forced the resignation of President Gotabaya Rajapaksa, which led to Ranil Wickremesinghe becoming president on July 21. .Read More..

Three fuel shipments due soon

Three fuel shipments are to reach Sri Lankan shores soon. Sri Lanka’s Power and Energy Minister Kanchana Wijesekra updated the delivery of fuel to Sri Lanka on Twitter. He said on Friday, full payments were made with the assistance of the Central Bank of Sri Lanka, Ministry of Finance to Lanka IOC for a Diesel Cargo to arrive between the 15th & 17th of July, and for Petrol Cargo for 22nd to 24th July.Read More..

120,000 MT of crude oil to reach SL on 29th

The Ceylon Petroleum Corporation (CPC) said that the unloading of 100,000 MT of crude oil from a vessel that reached Colombo. A senior official to the CPC said that accordingly, operations of the Sapugaskanda Oil Refinery will resume on the 19th of August. In addition, another vessel carrying 120,000 MT of crude oil is scheduled to reach the country on the 29th of August, the CPC said. Read More..

Limited Fuel Supply & Zero Gas distribution in Sri Lanka

The Ministry of Power and Energy said that it will release fuel while managing the existing stocks. The Ministry said that petrol distribution will be limited. The Sapugaskanda Petroleum Refinery has said that it will manage crude oil stocks until the 28th of June and release fuel. In the meantime, Litro gas noted that gas will NOT be distributed in the country. But people are still waiting in lines to purchase gas. Read More..

Sri Lankan Minister goes to CID against CPC

Minister of Power and Energy Kanchana Wijesekara said that he filed a complaint with the CID to investigate the activities at the Ceylon Petroleum Corporation, and the Ceylon Petroleum Storage Terminals Limited (CPSTL). Kanchana Wijesekera has requested to investigate Fuel procurement, Evaluation of proposals, Non-placement of orders, Selection of suppliers, Delays in payments, Distribution irregularities & Allegations made by individuals against the Ceylon Petroleum Corporation, and the Ceylon Petroleum Storage Terminals Limited (CPSTL).
